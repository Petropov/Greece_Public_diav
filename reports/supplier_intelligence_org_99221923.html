Platform detection capabilities — 9 tenets, what each surfaces
Reading guide: Each card describes a distinct procurement-irregularity use case the platform detects algorithmically from Diavgeia records. Red = fired at ΓΝ ΛΑΜΙΑ with evidence. Green = clean for that pattern. Platform blind spots (what cannot be detected) are listed below.
FIRED
T1 · Direct-award concentration
Use case: Preferred-vendor capture. One supplier absorbs a structurally disproportionate share of no-bid spending — indicates either exclusive technical dependence (which should trigger Art.32§2β, not §2γ), repeated supplier choice bias, or capture of the procurement officer making award decisions.
↳ AFM 094081864 (ISS/UNISON) = 26.6% of all ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ spend 2020–2026 (€3.26M, 8 awards). A commoditised cleaning service repeatedly awarded without competition.
FIRED
T4 · Amendment inflation / price carry
Use case: Price ratchet mechanism. Contract issued at €X → ΣΥΜΒΑΣΗ amendment raises to €X+δ → next contract starts at €X+δ as the "established rate," never re-tested against market. Consecutive ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ awards to the same supplier within 18 months with ≤5% price change and no intervening competitive tender.
↳ ISS/UNISON (AFM 094081864): Jul 2025 award €680,859 → Dec 2025 ΕΑΔΗΣΥ approval at identical €680,859. Zero price movement in 155 days with no market test. Rate locked in as baseline for next cycle.
FIRED
T5 · Emergency procurement overuse
Use case: Art.32§2γ misuse. The emergency negotiated procedure (no publication, no competition) is lawful only for genuinely unforeseeable urgent needs. A service that runs every year is foreseeable. Fires when annual emergency rate ≥15% OR any single award >€60k on emergency grounds — the latter being twice the Art.118 direct-award ceiling.
↳ 5 high-value emergency awards: €772,767 (Apr 2026), €680,859 (Dec 2025), €88,672 and €60,663 (2021 ICU consumables), €72,054 (2021 liquid oxygen). Cleaning service renewed as "emergency" every cycle for 6+ years.
FIRED
T6 · Temporal clustering
Use case: Three accountability-evasion patterns: (a) year-end budget burning — procurement volume 3–5× normal in Dec 22–31, decisions signed en masse before year close without adequate review; (b) weekend/holiday awards — decisions posted when oversight is absent; (c) monthly spikes — sudden single-month surges suggesting pre-prepared batch signing.
↳ Dec 22–31 burst every year 2022–2025 (143/145/116/83 decisions at 3.3–5.4× baseline). Weekend: €4.39M cleaning tender decision posted on Sunday 2023-12-17. 25 additional weekend/holiday findings.

T8 · Single-source monopoly
Use case: Annual capture. One supplier controls >70% of total direct-award spend in a calendar year (critical) or >50% for 2+ consecutive years (sustained monopoly). At a multi-service hospital with dozens of procurement categories, a 70%+ share of no-bid spend concentrated in one supplier is structurally anomalous.
↳ 2026: ISS/UNISON = 72.2% of all ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ spend — €1.74M out of €2.41M total. Three separate emergency awards for a single commodity service in one calendar year.

FIRED
T9C · 30-day award burst (lot-splitting)
Use case: Deliberate lot-splitting. Art.118 Law 4412/2016 caps direct awards at €30k per homogeneous need — not per transaction. Three or more awards to the same supplier within 30 days for the same service category circumvents this ceiling by fragmenting a single procurement need across multiple sub-threshold decisions.
↳ 14 burst windows. AFM 998768277: 6 awards in 15 days (Nov 2020) = €96,745 total for respiratory equipment — 3.2× the Art.118 ceiling. AFM 094027257: 4 awards in 25 days (Feb–Mar 2022) = €59,901 for immunological reagents.
FIRED
T9D · Price standardisation across suppliers
Use case: Fixed-rate manipulation or procurement coordination. The same non-round amount paid to 3 or more different suppliers. When identical odd prices appear across independent procurement events for different counterparties, it indicates either a mechanically applied rate schedule (no market testing) or coordination between nominally independent award decisions.
↳ 15 standardised price points. €4,960 paid to 5 different suppliers; €248 to 4 suppliers; €483.60 to 4 suppliers. Non-round amounts repeated across multiple counterparties indicate template-rate procurement.

Platform limitations — what this system cannot detect
Market price benchmarking
No external price reference database. Whether €680k/5-month for hospital cleaning is above or below market for a 307-bed hospital is unknown from Diavgeia alone. T4 detects frozen prices — not inflated prices. External market comparison requires tendering data from comparable hospitals or published rate surveys.
Beneficial ownership and shell companies
AFMs without ΓΕΜΗ registration or natural-person contractors cannot be automatically identified as shells or fronts. T1/T8 surface the concentration; verifying whether a supplier is a front for a connected party requires ΓΕΜΗ, UBO registry, and directorship cross-reference — manual steps outside Diavgeia scope.
Cross-year lot-splitting
T9C detects splitting within 30 days within the same calendar year. A procurement need split across the Dec 31 / Jan 1 boundary (Dec award + Jan award for the same homogeneous service) evades the detector. The Art.118 ceiling applies per-need regardless of year boundary under Law 4412/2016.
Delivery and performance verification
Diavgeia records the decision to award, not whether goods or services were actually delivered. A ghost supplier receiving €772k for cleaning can only be verified by building access logs or physical inspection records — entirely outside Diavgeia scope.
Revolving door and conflict of interest
Whether a procurement official has a financial interest in ISS/UNISON, or subsequently moves to work for the supplier, is undetectable from Diavgeia. Requires cross-reference with Πόθεν Έσχες income declarations and company directorship records (ΓΕΜΗ).
CPV misclassification
Intentional wrong-CPV filing hides contracts from category-based searches and audits. The platform detects this only incidentally when the same supplier uses the correct CPV elsewhere. Systematic misclassification for concealment requires CPV validity cross-checks not implemented in the current pipeline.
