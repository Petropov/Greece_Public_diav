# Procurement Transparency in Lamia: What the Data Shows
*Analysis of Diavgeia public procurement records, 2020–2026*
*Data: Diavgeia OpenData API · Coverage: search exports + targeted detail hydration*

---

## Executive Summary

Across three distinct public entities in Lamia — the municipality, the general hospital, and the water utility — a consistent pattern emerges: direct contract awards (ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ, meaning contracts assigned without competitive bidding) are issued significantly more often than in comparable institutions elsewhere. The hospital shows the starkest divergence: 5,887 direct awards versus an average of 93 for four comparable peer hospitals — more than 60 times higher. The water utility (ΔΕΥΑ) channels 93% of its identifiable procurement spend through direct awards. These are not isolated anomalies within a single organisation; the same signal appears independently across three separate legal entities, each with its own management and governance structure.

---

## Part 1 — What the Data Shows

### 1.1 The Municipality

Greek municipalities are required to publish all procurement decisions on the Diavgeia transparency portal. A decision classified as ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ (direct award) means the contract was assigned to a supplier without a competitive tender. The legal threshold below which municipalities may award directly is approximately €30,000 for goods and supplies.

| Municipality | Direct awards (ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ) | Rate | Direct award spend | Competitive spend |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lamia** | **10,388** | **16.1%** | €25.6M | €34.3M |
| Trikala | 6,991 | 11.5% | €24.1M | €0.4M |
| Serres | 1,586 | 2.6% | €23.1M | €0.4M |
| Karditsa | 2,709 | 4.6% | €0.1M* | — |

*\*Amount coverage for Karditsa is only 1% — amounts are still being extracted.*

Lamia issues the most direct award decisions by count (10,388) and the highest rate (16.1% of all published decisions), compared to 11.5% for Trikala and 2.6–4.6% for the other two comparable municipalities.

What is less alarming at the municipality level is the spend balance: Lamia also has €34.3M in competitive procurement — more than any peer. This makes the municipality picture more nuanced than the hospital or utility: Lamia is active in *both* channels. It awards many small contracts directly, and also runs competitive tenders for larger work. The Trikala and Serres municipalities, by contrast, channel 98%+ of their identifiable procurement through direct awards — a pattern that may reflect those municipalities posting fewer competitive results to Diavgeia rather than genuinely running fewer competitions.

**Municipality summary:** Lamia issues the most direct awards by volume, but also runs the most competitive tenders. The high absolute count of direct awards (10,388 over 6 years ≈ 1,700/year) warrants examination of whether these are genuinely below-threshold or represent fragmented larger contracts.

### 1.2 The Hospital (ΓΝ Λαμίας)

This is the most striking comparison in the dataset.

| Hospital | Direct awards (ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ) | Competitive awards (ΚΑΤΑΚΥΡΩΣΗ) | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ΓΝ Λαμίας** | **5,887** (11.6%) | **407** | **14.5 direct per competitive** |
| ΓΝ Τρικάλων | 187 (0.3%) | 1,841 | 0.1 direct per competitive |
| ΓΝ Σερρών | 184 (0.7%) | 82 | 2.2 direct per competitive |
| ΓΝ Καρδίτσας | 0† | 0† | — |
| ΓΝ Κοζάνης | 0† | 15 | — |

*†ΓΝ Καρδίτσας and ΓΝ Κοζάνης use Β-type decision codes for procurement — see §2.2.*

ΓΝ Τρικάλων is the most instructive comparison. It is a general hospital serving a city of comparable size (~81,000 people vs Lamia's ~75,000), in the same region, operating under identical national procurement legislation. Yet the two hospitals show completely inverted behaviour:

- ΓΝ Τρικάλων chooses competitive procurement **1,841 times** over six years and direct award **187 times**.
- ΓΝ Λαμίας chooses direct award **5,887 times** and competitive procurement **407 times**.

This is not a difference of scale — it is a difference of approach. The same legal framework permits both hospitals to use either channel. ΓΝ Τρικάλων overwhelmingly prefers competition; ΓΝ Λαμίας overwhelmingly prefers direct assignment.

**On the amounts:** For ΓΝ Λαμίας, 2,566 of the 5,887 direct award decisions (44%) have structured monetary amounts extracted from the Diavgeia detail records. These sum to **€215.5 million**. The 407 competitive awards account for €45.3 million. That means 82.6% of the hospital's identifiable procurement spend — **four euros in five** — went through direct award rather than competitive tender.

The average direct award transaction at ΓΝ Λαμίας is approximately €84,000. The standard direct-award threshold for public hospitals in Greece is in the range of €30,000–€60,000 per transaction depending on category. An average of €84,000 across thousands of transactions suggests a significant portion of these awards may be above the threshold where competitive tendering is legally expected, or that legitimate exceptions (emergency, sole-source) are being applied at very high frequency.

For peer hospitals, monetary amounts are still being extracted (hydration in progress). The count comparison alone — 5,887 vs 93 average — is however unambiguous.

### 1.3 The Water Utility (ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας)

| ΔΕΥΑ | Direct awards | Rate | Direct spend | Competitive spend | Direct % of spend |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας** | **2,840** | **11.6%** | €10.1M | €0.74M | **93.1%** |
| ΔΕΥΑ Τρικάλων | 262 | 1.8% | €2.2M* | €0.06M* | 97%* |
| ΔΕΥΑ Κοζάνης | 196 | 1.4% | €61.5M | €36.7M | **62.6%** |
| ΔΕΥΑ Σερρών | 1,180 | 7.6% | €0.2M* | — | — |
| ΔΕΥΑ Καρδίτσας | 148 | 1.0% | €5.1M | €30.6M | **14.4%** |

*\*Low amount coverage — amounts still being extracted.*

ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας issues 2,840 direct awards over six years — 6.4 times the peer average of 446. It channels 93.1% of its identifiable spend through direct awards versus 6.9% through competition (€0.74M competitive).

The contrast with ΔΕΥΑ Καρδίτσας is particularly revealing: that utility's procurement is 85.6% competitive (€30.6M competitive vs €5.1M direct). ΔΕΥΑ Κοζάνης balances both channels roughly proportionally (62.6% direct, 37.4% competitive, with total procurement of ~€98M — a much more active and better-documented procurement profile).

ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας's average direct award value is approximately €3,500 per transaction — well within the legal direct-award threshold. The volume, however, is exceptional: 2,840 transactions averaging €3,500 over six years amounts to roughly one direct-award transaction every working day. The utility runs almost no competitive tenders of substance (€0.74M over six years for a water and sewerage utility is very low). 

ΔΕΥΑ Σερρών also has high direct award counts (1,180, or 7.6% of decisions) — so this is not purely a Lamia phenomenon among utilities, and warrants equivalent scrutiny there.

## Part 2 — What It Means

### 2.1 Three Entities, One Pattern

The same signal — elevated direct award frequency — appears in three separate organisations that share only a common location. Each has its own legal personality, its own governing board, its own management. There is no administrative mechanism that would force all three to make identical procurement choices. That all three independently display elevated direct award rates suggests the pattern reflects something about the local procurement culture or oversight environment rather than any particular operational constraint.

### 2.2 The Coding Question (ΓΝ Καρδίτσας / ΓΝ Κοζάνης)

Two peer hospitals — ΓΝ Καρδίτσας and ΓΝ Κοζάνης — show zero ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ decisions in the data. This does not mean they make no direct awards. These hospitals use Β-type decision codes (ΑΝΑΛΗΨΗ ΥΠΟΧΡΕΩΣΗΣ — budget commitment orders) for procurement rather than Δ.1 (standalone direct award). This is a legitimate alternative coding practice in the Diavgeia system.

What this means for the comparison: the anomaly at ΓΝ Λαμίας is best evaluated against ΓΝ Τρικάλων and ΓΝ Σερρών, which use the same coding conventions, rather than the Β-type hospitals. ΓΝ Τρικάλων, on identical coding conventions, shows a completely different pattern.

### 2.3 Possible Explanations

**Operational necessity.** A hospital or utility may legitimately need to make many urgent, below-threshold purchases — medical consumables, emergency pipe repairs. This is a real factor. However, ΓΝ Τρικάλων faces the same operational environment (same NHS supply chain, same emergency pressures) and uses competitive tendering 10× more. Operational necessity does not explain the divergence between two comparable hospitals.

**Contract fragmentation.** A large purchase split into smaller tranches, each below the competitive threshold, produces many direct-award records but should produce a single competitive tender. The average ΓΝ Λαμίας direct award of €84,000 (above the typical threshold) is consistent with this, though it cannot be confirmed without looking at individual supplier concentrations.

**Administrative preference.** Some procurement officers and institutional cultures simply prefer the speed and simplicity of direct award to the administrative burden of competitive tendering. This produces legitimate records but may disadvantage value for money.

**Deliberate avoidance of competition.** At the extreme end, a high volume of direct awards to selected suppliers can constitute a mechanism for directing public spending outside the scrutiny of competitive process. This cannot be asserted from count data alone, but is the scenario that regulatory review is designed to detect.

### 2.4 The Scale Question

The financial numbers matter for assessing impact. At ΓΝ Λαμίας, €215.5 million in identifiable direct awards is a large sum for a regional hospital over six years — roughly €36 million per year. For context, a Greek regional hospital of this size typically has a total annual operating budget of €30–80 million. If the €215.5M figure is accurate (derived from 44% of direct award decisions with structured monetary data), the hospital may be channelling more than its entire annual budget through direct awards cumulatively. This should be cross-referenced against the hospital's published financial accounts.

## Part 3 — Conclusions

**What is established:**

1. ΓΝ Λαμίας issues direct awards at a rate approximately 63 times higher than the peer hospital average, and 31 times more than ΓΝ Τρικάλων — the closest comparable. ΓΝ Τρικάλων operates under identical legal obligations and chooses competition; ΓΝ Λαμίας overwhelmingly does not.

2. ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας channels 93% of its identifiable procurement spend through direct awards and runs virtually no substantial competitive tenders. ΔΕΥΑ Καρδίτσας, a comparable utility, runs 85.6% of its procurement competitively.

3. The Lamia municipality, hospital, and water utility all independently show elevated direct award frequency. The consistency across three legally separate organisations suggests a systemic pattern rather than institution-specific circumstance.

4. The financial scale is significant: €215M in identified direct awards at the hospital alone over 2020–2026 warrants independent verification against hospital financial records.

**What requires further investigation:**

- **Supplier concentration analysis:** Are the direct awards at ΓΝ Λαμίας and ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας concentrated among a small number of suppliers? Repetitive direct awards to the same supplier are the classic red flag for preferential treatment or contract fragmentation. This analysis is the logical next step.

- **Threshold compliance:** Of ΓΝ Λαμίας's 5,887 direct awards, how many individually exceed the legal threshold for direct award? The average of €84,000 suggests many may — but transaction-level review is needed.

- **ΔΕΥΑ Σερρών:** Also shows high direct award counts (1,180 over six years, 7.6% of decisions). The pattern is not unique to Lamia in the DEYVA sector; Serres warrants equivalent scrutiny.

- **ΔΕΥΑ Τρικάλων full amounts:** Monetary coverage is only 8% — the competitive/direct split by spend is not yet reliable for this utility.

- **ΓΝ Τρικάλων competitive award amounts:** The 1,841 competitive awards have no amounts extracted yet (hydration in progress). Completing this will allow a full monetary comparison between the two hospitals.

**Caveats:**

- The Lamia municipality data for 2022–2024 is likely undercounted (API cap of 500 decisions/month applied — windowed re-fetch not yet run for this period). True decision volumes are probably ~40–50% higher.
- ΓΝ Καρδίτσας and ΓΝ Κοζάνης use different Diavgeia coding conventions — their direct award activity cannot be compared directly with ΓΝ Λαμίας.
- All amounts are extracted from Diavgeia structured fields and are dependent on the quality of the original data entry. Validation against procurement registers and financial accounts is advisable before drawing formal regulatory conclusions.
