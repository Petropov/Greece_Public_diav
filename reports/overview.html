Procurement Transparency in Lamia

A cross-entity comparison of direct contract awards across the municipality, general hospital, and water utility — measured against comparable institutions in Trikala, Serres, Karditsa and Kozani.

📅 January 2020 – May 2026 🏛️ 14 public entities analysed 📄 ~560,000 decisions processed Source: diavgeia.gov.gr
Key finding: Three separate public entities in Lamia — the municipality, the general hospital (ΓΝ Λαμίας), and the water utility (ΔΕΥΑ) — independently show elevated rates of direct contract award relative to peer institutions. ΓΝ Λαμίας issues direct awards 63× more often than the peer hospital average. ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας channels 93% of its procurement spend through direct awards.

The Trikala Inversion

ΓΝ Τρικάλων is the most instructive comparison: same city size (~81,000 people), same type of general hospital, same national procurement law. Yet it issues 1,841 competitive tenders and only 187 direct awards — the exact mirror of ΓΝ Λαμίας (5,887 direct, 407 competitive). The same legal framework produces opposite behaviour. This is a choice, not a constraint.

Two Models of Utility Procurement

ΔΕΥΑ Καρδίτσας channels 85.6% of its procurement through competitive tendering (€30.6M in competitive awards vs €5.1M direct). ΔΕΥΑ Κοζάνης runs a large and transparent procurement programme (€98M total) with both channels actively used. ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας runs 2,840 direct awards averaging ~€3,500 each — roughly one per working day — and almost no competitive tenders of substance (€0.74M over six years).

More Nuanced Picture at Municipal Level

Lamia municipality issues the most direct award decisions (10,388 at 16.1% rate), but also runs 214 competitive tenders worth €34.3M — far more competitive procurement by value than any peer municipality. Trikala and Serres channel 98%+ of spend through direct awards but have far fewer transactions. Lamia's profile suggests high-volume small direct purchases alongside large competitive contracts — both ends of the spectrum. The 2022–2024 figures for Lamia are likely undercounted (~40–50%) due to an API retrieval gap not yet corrected.

Systemic Signal, Not Isolated Incident

The elevated direct-award rate appears independently at three legally separate Lamia entities — the municipality (16.1%), the hospital (11.6%), and the water utility (11.6%). Each has its own governing board, management, and procurement officers. No administrative mechanism forces all three to make the same procurement choices. The consistent signal across independent organisations points to a systemic characteristic of the local procurement environment rather than any single institutional failure.

6Conclusions & Next Steps

📊

What is established

  • ΓΝ Λαμίας: 63× more direct awards than peer hospital average
  • ΓΝ Τρικάλων (same size, same law) chooses competition 10× more
  • ΔΕΥΑ Λαμίας: 93% of spend via direct award; ΔΕΥΑ Καρδίτσας: 14%
  • Same pattern at municipality, hospital, and utility independently
  • €215.5M in identified direct award spend at ΓΝ Λαμίας (2020–2026)
🔍

Next steps that matter

  • Supplier concentration: who receives the direct awards? Repeated awards to the same supplier = fragmentation or preference
  • Threshold compliance: avg ΓΝ Λαμίας direct award = €84K, above the ~€30–60K legal threshold
  • ΔΕΥΑ Σερρών: also elevated (1,180 direct awards) — warrants same scrutiny
  • Cross-reference financials: €215M vs published hospital accounts
⚠️

Caveats

  • ΑΝΑΘΕΣΗ includes emergency & sole-source awards — not all are irregular
  • ΓΝ Καρδίτσας / ΓΝ Κοζάνης use different Diavgeia coding (Β-types)
  • Municipality 2022–2024 data is ~40–50% undercounted (API cap)
  • Peer hospital amounts still being extracted (hydration in progress)
  • Data quality validation against procurement registers recommended
Methodology note: All data is sourced from the Diavgeia public procurement transparency portal (diavgeia.gov.gr) via the OpenData API.
